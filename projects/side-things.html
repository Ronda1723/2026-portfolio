CASE STUDY · 04 2025 — NOW

Side things

Weekend experiments. The ones that don't graduate yet.

A drawer of small tools — half of them prove a hunch, half of them prove I was wrong. Both kinds are useful. Here are the ones currently in the workshop.

SpaceX 3D portfolio

A voice-commanded 3D portfolio with a Blender MCP bridge. You say "show me the Falcon," and a model loads and orients itself in real time. Mostly a test rig for: can MCP make 3D tooling feel like conversation?

Answer: yes for spatial primitives, no for fine-grained material work. Voice is great at "show me," not at "make the bump map 0.04 stronger."

Weekend MCPs

Small Model Context Protocol servers wrapping personal tools — a journal indexer, a "find that paper I half-read" search, a Linear-but-private clone. Each takes a Saturday. None of them are products. Together they shape what I want a "Company Brain" to feel like.

Why I keep doing this

Side projects are taste calibration. They let me find out what feels right at small scale before committing to it at product scale. The 3D voice command, the per-employee memory shape, the latency obsessions — all of those started as a Saturday.

If something here ever graduates, it'll show up as a card on the front page.
