The shape of the problem

Voice agents fail in one of two ways: they sound robotic, or they pause too long. Both are about latency budgets. The user's brain decides the agent is "broken" within ~600ms of silence — long before any model can fully compose a response.

The win isn't a smarter model. It's tighter plumbing: streaming partial transcripts, predictive endpointing, jitter-tolerant WebSocket frames, and TTS that starts speaking before generation finishes.

What I worked on

  • STT streaming with predictive endpoint detection
  • TTS streaming with chunk-level playback
  • Jitter buffers calibrated to mobile vs desktop network profiles
  • WebSocket fan-out for multi-tenant call routing
  • Observability — where milliseconds disappear, and why

The lesson I'm taking forward

Latency is not a number — it's a feel. Two systems with identical average latency can feel completely different if their variance differs. A consistent 280ms response feels better than a system that swings between 50ms and 600ms with the same average. Half the work was on the tail of the distribution, not the median.
